About

Our map application goes beyond providing data—it fosters a community of contributors and environmental stewards. By combining vital datasets on biodiversity and environmental risks with an interactive blogging platform, we invite users to not only explore but also share their discoveries, insights, and conservation ideas. Whether you are a scientist, activist, or nature enthusiast, this map-based tool empowers you to contribute to the collective understanding of Canada’s ecological landscape.

Analysis

The application integrates various datasets, enabling users to delve into key aspects of biodiversity and environmental protection. Additionally, the blogging feature opens the platform for public contributions, promoting dialogue and knowledge-sharing on critical issues such as habitat loss, species conservation, and wildfire risk management. Key features include:

Protected Species Map

Visualize animals and plants listed under Canada’s conservation efforts - This feature helps users explore where endangered species reside and highlights regions where efforts to protect biodiversity are most needed.

Critical Habitat Map

Identify the areas crucial for endangered and threatened species. Users can interact with this layer to learn where key habitat zones are located, helping conservationists, policymakers, and the public focus on safeguarding these essential areas.

Vegetation Zones

Look at Canada's Vegetation Zones, essential for understanding the diversity of plant life that forms the foundation of ecosystems. By exploring different vegetation types, users gain insights into how these zones contribute to the health and balance of wildlife populations.

Wildfire Hotspots

This feature uses historical data to map fire-prone regions across Canada. This feature helps users visualize areas at risk, track ongoing wildfires, and understand their impact on habitats and ecosystems.

Priority Species in Canada

Discover Canada's priority species needing conservation to promote environmental awareness and community action.
